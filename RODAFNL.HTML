Welcome to Palawan!

The Philippines' Last Ecological Frontier

About Palawan

Palawan is an archipelagic province in the Philippines located in the Mimaropa region. It is the largest province in the country in terms of total area of jurisdiction, with its capital city being Puerto Princesa.

Known as the "Last Frontier" of the Philippines, Palawan is home to stunning natural wonders, diverse wildlife, and some of the most beautiful islands in the world. The province boasts two UNESCO World Heritage Sites: the Puerto Princesa Underground River and the Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park.

Top Destinations

  • El Nido - Famous for its limestone cliffs, hidden beaches, and stunning lagoons
  • Coron - Known for its World War II wreck diving sites and breathtaking lakes
  • Puerto Princesa Underground River - One of the New 7 Wonders of Nature
  • Port Barton - A quieter alternative to El Nido with beautiful beaches
  • San Vicente - Home to the 14km-long Long Beach
  • Taytay - Features the historic Fuerza de Santa Isabel fortress

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Palawan is during the dry season from November to May. The peak tourist season is from December to April when the weather is at its best. The rainy season from June to October can bring heavy rains and occasional typhoons, though this is also when you'll find fewer tourists and lower prices.

How to Get There

Palawan has three main airports with international and domestic flights:

  • Puerto Princesa International Airport - The main gateway with the most flight options
  • El Nido Airport - Serves direct flights from Manila and Cebu
  • Francisco B. Reyes Airport (Coron) - Also called Busuanga Airport

Ferries also connect Palawan to other parts of the Philippines, particularly from Manila and Iloilo.

Travel Tips

  • Book accommodations and tours in advance during peak season
  • Bring reef-safe sunscreen to protect marine life
  • Pack light, breathable clothing and good walking shoes
  • Carry cash as ATMs can be limited in smaller towns
  • Respect local customs and environmental regulations
  • Try local specialties like Tamilok (woodworm) and crocodile sisig

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions

General Questions

What is the best time to visit Palawan?

The dry season from November to May is ideal, with peak season from December to April offering the best weather.

Do I need a visa to visit Palawan?

Visa requirements depend on your nationality. Most visitors can enter the Philippines visa-free for stays up to 30 days.

Travel Planning

How many days should I spend in Palawan?

We recommend at least 5-7 days to experience the highlights. Two weeks allows for a more relaxed exploration.

What's the best way to get around Palawan?

For longer distances, flights between major destinations are fastest. Ferries and buses connect other areas. In towns, tricycles are common.

Booking & Payments

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept major credit cards, PayPal, and bank transfers. Some local providers may require cash payments.

Can I make changes to my booking?

Changes are subject to availability and may incur fees. Contact us as soon as possible to request changes.

Health & Safety

Are vaccinations required for Palawan?

No specific vaccinations are required, but Hepatitis A, Typhoid, and Tetanus are recommended. Consult your doctor before traveling.

Is Palawan safe for tourists?

Palawan is generally very safe. Exercise normal precautions against petty theft and be mindful of natural hazards when exploring.
